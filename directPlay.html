Direct Play

Learn about the direct play style of soccer and its tactical advantages

What is Direct Play?

Direct play (also known as 'kickball') is the simplest tactic in soccer. Most people who have played soccer, whether it was in a recreational league or in a club team, have more than likely played in a system with some form of direct play.

The Idea Behind Direct Play

The idea behind direct play is to simply get the ball towards the opponent's goal as quickly as possible. This often involves long balls or through balls, or having more skillful players dribble the ball up the pitch.

Advantages

The main advantage of direct play is that it is very simple to understand. It is easy to teach to new players and it is easy to play.

Disadvantages

Since teams are not concerned with possession of the ball, teams are often not able to keep the ball for long periods of time. This is the biggest disadvantage of direct play.

Key Characteristics of Direct Play

    This style is well suited for fast and physical teams.
    Players must be willing to play physically and win aerial duels for this style to be effective.
    On defense, players will typically press the ball aggressively to win the ball back as fast as possible.
    On offense, players will kick the ball forward in as little passes possible. This typically involves a long ball (a high kick over the defense) or a through ball (a pass on the ground that breaks the opponent's line).
    Target players: Since the ball is hit up the field as fast as possible, teams usually require target players who are either very fast or very strong (or both) to be able to control the ball up the field.
