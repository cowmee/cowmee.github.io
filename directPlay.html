Direct Play

Learn about the direct play style of soccer and its tactical advantages

What is Direct Play?

Direct play (also known as 'kickball') is the simplest tactic in soccer. Most people who have played soccer, whether it was in a recreational league or in a club team, have more than likely played in a system with some form of direct play.

The idea behind direct play is to simply get the ball towards the opponent's goal as quickly as possible. This often involves long balls or through balls that travel across the field. The image shown on the left shows a typical direct play attacking idea. The player on the ball is looking to find the highest option (usually the target player), which is shown by the yellow arrow. The other two arrows show players using their speed to get into the best position to receive the second ball.

Advantages

The main advantage of direct play is that it is very simple to understand. It is easy to teach to new players and it is easy to play. This diagram is faily simple, and it is because direct play is really that simple. Just find the highest option and run onto the ball.

Disadvantages

Since teams are not concerned with possession of the ball, teams are often not able to keep the ball for long periods of time. This is the biggest disadvantage of direct play.

In this example, we can see that the player on the ball is always looking for the highest option. In a perfect world, this ball would be easy to play. However, since this is a long pass to make, it puts the team at risk of losing the ball, due to an interception, a bad touch by the recieving player, or a bad pass by the passer.

Key Characteristics of Direct Play

  • This style is well suited for fast and physical teams.
  • Players must be willing to play physically and win aerial duels for this style to be effective.
  • On defense, players will typically press the ball aggressively to win the ball back as fast as possible.
  • On offense, players will kick the ball forward in as little passes possible. This typically involves a long ball (a high kick over the defense) or a through ball (a pass on the ground that breaks the opponent's line).
  • Target players: Since the ball is hit up the field as fast as possible, teams usually require target players who are either very fast or very strong (or both) to be able to control the ball up the field.

Direct Play in Action

Burnley FC

Under Sean Dyche, Burnley became known for their direct style of play in the Premier League. They often used long balls to target forwards like Chris Wood and Ashley Barnes, who were excellent at holding up play and winning aerial duels.

This approach allowed them to compete against teams with much larger budgets and establish themselves in the top flight of English football.
